Brief Profile

Velagapudi Ramakrishna Siddhartha Engineering College was established in 1977 as the first private self-financing engineering college in the state of composite Andhra Pradesh and is sponsored by “Siddhartha Academy of General and Technical Education”, a registered society run by philanthropists from different walks of life. The college is in autonomous status granted by UGC in 2006. The college is the first technical institution in Andhra Pradesh to secure an extension of autonomous status without physical visits for a decade at a stretch up to 2027-2028. The college is offering B.Tech. programme in 9 branches with an intake of 1260, Seven M.Tech. programs with 90, in addition to 60 each in MBA and MCA programs. At present, the college has 315 well-experienced faculty members including 149 members with PhD qualifications. The college is spread over 24.05 acres of land with a built-up area of 66,165 sq.m, meeting all the norms of AICTE, and is permanently affiliated to JNTUK, Kakinada.

Siddhartha Academy, the Fountain head

Siddhartha Academy of General and Technical Education had been formed in 1975 by a team of 250 philanthropists with a vision for the future to set up and promote such educational institutions of excellence that would reorient general, technical, and professional education with a holistic approach. Assimilating the advances made in various domains of knowledge and considering the educational needs of the region, 15 academic institutions from KG to PG which include Public schools(2), Arts & Science colleges(3), Engineering colleges(2), Pharmacy college, Medical college, Dental college, School of Nursing, College of Nursing, Law college, College of Education, Hotel Management & Catering Technology and a couple of Service & Cultural organizations with 3,000 teaching & nonteaching staff and 23,500 students have been established.

In addition, the Academic has been gloriously rendering services to the Indian Heritage & Culture through Siddhartha Kalapeetham and supporting the deserving poor students through the Siddhartha Foundation.

Institution’s Innovation Council

Ministry of Education (MoE), Govt. of India has established ‘MoE’s Innovation Cell (MIC)’ to systematically foster the culture of Innovation amongst all Higher Education Institutions (HEIs). The primary mandate of MIC is to encourage, inspire, and nurture young students by supporting them to work with new ideas and transform them into prototypes while they are informative years. MIC has envisioned encouraging the creation of ‘Institution’s Innovation Councils (IICs)’ across selected HEIs. A network of these IICs will be established to promote innovation in the Institution through multitudinous modes leading to an innovation promotion eco-system in the campuses.
